Here are the most popular ingredients for each cuisine. You can hover over bars and see which ingredients are popular across cuisines. For example, who uses a lot of soy sauce? Who uses a lot of butter?




We then created ingredient count vectors for each cuisine, reweighted them using tf-idf, and plotted them on two dimensions using singular value decomposition. We can see that cuisines in the same continent are closer to each other.




Co-occurrence is measured by a metric called normalized pointwise mutual information (NPMI), which helps account for the overall frequency of ingredients (so that popular ingredients such as salt don't dominant everything). This graph is a network of 922 ingredients that occur at least 50 times in our dataset, and only shows edges where the NPMI between ingredients is above 0.25.

Zoom in to see node labels, and hover over nodes to see their neighbors. Can you find the section of the graph that focuses on ingredients used in desserts? What are some common ingredient pairs that you've also used in your own cooking?
